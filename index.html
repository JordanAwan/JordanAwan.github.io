Bio

I studied at Clarion University from 2011-2014, earning a B.S. in Mathematics. After this, I completed a M.A. in Mathematics at Brandeis University in 2016 under the advisement of Dr. Olivier Bernardi. In May of 2020, I completed my Ph.D. in Statistics at Penn State University, advised by Dr. Aleksandra Slavkovic and Dr. Matthew Reimherr. Currently I am an Assistant Professor of Statistics at Purdue University. I also work as a differential privacy consultant for the federal non-profit, MITRE.

My research interests lie in a variety of areas, including My research expertise is on data privacy, with a focus on differential privacy. Some of my work has studied problems in the areas of functional data analysis, classical statistical inference, and empirical risk minimization under the constraint of differential privacy. I also work on applied problems related to pitch tracking for the analysis of physiological signals, and I have previously worked on discrete mathematics problems.
